Back to the Grind

IN WEEKLY BLOGS ON 21ST - 25TH APR 2025

After a refreshing break, I returned to the NSTP Office feeling recharged and ready to get back to work. The moment I stepped into the office, I could already feel the momentum picking up as the staff resumed their daily routines. I was immediately given a list of tasks to work on, including encoding several sets of school documents and printing out necessary forms that had been lined up before the break. It felt good to be trusted with important responsibilities again, especially after the temporary pause. I took my time to review the instructions given to me and ensured that I followed them correctly while staying efficient. The familiar clacking of the keyboard and the hum of the printer brought me back into rhythm, and it was like I had never left. I also double-checked the printouts to make sure everything looked neat and accurate before handing them over. Throughout the week, I made it a point to keep my workspace clean and organized, something I had grown used to during the earlier weeks of my OJT. It was satisfying to know that I could handle my tasks with more confidence now. Getting back to the grind felt like a good reminder of how much I had grown in the past few weeks.

Most of the documents I worked on this week were related to NSTP internal reports and other school paperwork that needed to be finalized before the semester's end. I continued encoding drafts of official communications, formatted them based on instructions, and helped prepare printed copies for submission. One of my favorite things about this week was the sense of productivity that came from having a full schedule again. There was never a dull moment in the office, and I liked being busy with tasks that contributed to the smooth flow of operations.
