Preparing for a Pause

IN WEEKLY BLOGS ON 7TH - 11TH APR 2025

This week felt like another opportunity to keep the positive momentum going in my internship journey. After finding my rhythm in the previous weeks, I focused once again on the usual tasks like encoding, printing, and preparing office documents needed for NSTP operations. I continued to handle various files that were mostly related to school processes, such as reports, forms, and meeting notes. Each day, I took time to organize the soft copies on the computer, making sure that every document was saved in the right folder and labeled clearly. One of the things I really appreciated was how every small task connected to the larger goals of the department. Whether it was printing a batch of reports or double-checking paper supplies, everything I did contributed to keeping the office running smoothly. I liked how organized my days had become, and I found comfort in the consistency of my routine. Having a checklist helped me stay on top of my duties, and it felt good whenever I ticked off a task as completed. These little accomplishments reminded me how much I had grown since my first week.

I also assisted with printing handouts and other documents needed for in-campus activities and briefings. There were moments when I was asked to help carry and arrange printed materials in preparation for internal meetings or small gatherings held at the school. While these tasks might seem simple, I realized how important they were for making sure that faculty and staff had everything they needed. I continued making digital backups of several documents that had been printed, helping ensure that files were safe and easy to access in the future. There were also times when I was asked to format documents more neatly or resize print layouts so they would appear more professional. Doing these tasks allowed me to practice using tools I had learned in school while gaining new tips from the staff. The office remained busy, but it was a good kind of busy. Everyone worked with purpose, and it made me feel good to be included in that flow. I even helped arrange some folders and recheck stacks of papers to ensure they were correctly grouped and ready for review.

Looking back at this week, I felt proud of the reliability I had built within the office. I was becoming someone the staff could count on, and that gave me a strong sense of motivation.
