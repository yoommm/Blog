Improving Step-by-Step

IN WEEKLY BLOGS ON 17TH - 21ST FEB 2025

By the time I entered my third week at the NSTP Office, I was already feeling more confident with my usual responsibilities and ready to take on additional tasks. I continued with my encoding duties, which had now become part of my daily routine. This week, I was introduced to organizing digital files related specifically to NSTP student records and activity documentation. At first, I thought it would be as simple as just dragging files into folders, but I soon learned that organizing digital records required a keen eye for detail and consistency in naming conventions and file structure. It was important to follow a specific system so that files could be easily retrieved later on by the staff or faculty. I also got to assist in preparing documents that were scheduled for submission, which included checking the printed materials for completeness and accuracy. This task required double-checking details like student names, dates, and signatures to ensure everything was in order before finalizing. I appreciated how the office staff trusted me with these responsibilities, and I did my best to meet their expectations. The process was a bit repetitive at times, but it helped sharpen my focus and taught me the value of patience and precision. These tasks also gave me a greater understanding of how proper documentation plays a crucial role in making sure NSTP activities are well-tracked and reported.

Aside from my clerical work, I was also introduced to a new and fun responsibility this week taking photos for documentation. It was my first time being part of the office’s effort to visually archive activities, and I found it both exciting and meaningful. I was asked to take photos of minor events and day-to-day office activities that would later be used for internal records or reports. At first, I felt a little nervous about capturing the right moments, especially since I wasn’t used to taking photos in a formal or office-related setting. But the staff guided me on what kinds of photos were useful and how to take them properly. I started to understand the importance of documentation not just in written form, but also in visual content. These images served as proof of completed activities and helped tell the story of what was happening within the office and the NSTP program. It made me realize how diverse office responsibilities can be one day I could be organizing spreadsheets, and the next day I could be taking pictures of an activity. This variety kept things interesting and allowed me to explore different skills. I also enjoyed the creativity involved in framing shots, capturing candid moments, and contributing something unique to the team. Little by little, I was discovering that there were many ways to support the office beyond just paperwork.

As the week progressed, I found myself becoming more proactive with how I handled tasks and offered help. I no longer waited to be told what to do instead, I started asking questions and volunteering for small assignments whenever I saw something that needed to be done. I was beginning to feel like part of the team, and not just someone temporarily assigned there. I noticed how small habits I picked up during the previous weeks, like organizing my space and double-checking my work, were now becoming second nature. My communication with the staff improved as well, as I became more comfortable speaking up and giving updates about the progress of my tasks. This week also helped me realize that good office work isn’t only about doing your job it’s also about building trust, being consistent, and showing initiative. Each task, no matter how simple, contributed to a bigger goal, and that gave me a deeper sense of purpose. I felt proud seeing the results of my efforts becoming part of the office’s official records and documents. It was a great feeling to be relied on, even for small things. Week 3 showed me how much I had already grown in just a short amount of time, and it made me excited for the challenges and learning opportunities in the coming weeks.
